      Methodology & health warnings

      This is an illustrative, directional model — a game, not an official fiscal costing. It uses a static representative-borrower approach: the workforce is split into twelve salary bands from ONS ASHE 2025 full-time earnings percentiles, scaled by a 1.125 graduate premium, and repayments are computed as 9% of income above the threshold, per year, for the full term, weighted across bands and multiplied by the 2022/23 cohort of 108,340 borrowers (DfE/LEO). The baseline weighted average lifetime repayment of £57,583 calibrates within 2.8% of the IFS benchmark of £56,000.

      Big caveats: earnings are held flat for life (no career progression), loan balances, interest and write-offs aren't simulated, behaviour doesn't change, and all figures are undiscounted cash for a single cohort — so "cash neutral" is not "fiscally neutral", because repayments pushed into years 31–40 are worth less in today's money. Real-world cost equivalents are rough public-domain ballparks for scale, not procurement quotes. Plan 2 rules as at April 2026: threshold £29,385, rate 9%, 30-year write-off.
